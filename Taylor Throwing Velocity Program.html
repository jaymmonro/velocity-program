These principles are drawn from research-backed softball throwing programs. When in doubt, mechanics first, load second. Never throw through pain.
⚡
Intent Is Everything
Research shows throwing at 90-100% intent is required to build velocity. Lazy throws build lazy arm speed. Every max-effort throw should feel like Taylor is trying to throw through the catcher's mitt, not just to it. Cue: "Rip through the ball."
⚖️
Overload + Underload Balance
Use the 2:1 ratio: two heavy ball throws for every regular-ball throw. Underload (100g) trains fast-twitch arm speed. Overload (250-450g) builds strength and deceleration. Together, they expand the velocity envelope on both ends.
🦵
Velocity Starts With the Legs
Outfield throwing velocity is generated from the ground up: foot, hip, torso, shoulder, arm. The crow-hop must be aggressive and rhythmic. Uphill shoulders during weight load, explosive hip fire, and a long stride all add mph before the arm ever fires.
🎯
Glove Arm is a Velocity Tool
The glove arm acts as a crank. When pulled powerfully back and down through the hip rotation, it accelerates the throwing arm. Most youth players leave 2-4 mph on the table here. Cue: "Pull the glove into your hip."
🔄
Pronation = Safe Arm
Teach Taylor to pronate through release (thumb rotates down). This is both a velocity mechanism and an injury-prevention tool. Supination (throwing off the side) bleeds speed and stresses the elbow. Cue: "Show your thumb to the ground after release."
📏
Long Toss on a Line
Long toss is most effective when thrown on a low, flat trajectory a cutoff man could intercept. High-arc long toss increases elbow stress without the same velocity benefit. The body learns to throw hard and efficient when chasing distance on a line.
🛡️
Arm Care is Non-Negotiable
Before every session: band external rotations (2x15), internal rotations (2x12), Ws (2x12), shoulder circles. After every session: cross-body stretch, sleeper stretch, forearm flexor/extensor stretch. Skip warmup = skip gains + add injury risk.
📊
Measure Consistently
Use the same setup for every velocity reading: full crow-hop from 55-60 ft, on flat ground, from the pulldown position. Pocket Radar app or a dedicated radar gun. Inconsistent measurement conditions create misleading data. Log every reading here.
🚫
Red Flags: Stop and Rest
If Taylor reports: sharp elbow pain, numbness in fingers, or shoulder pain with arm elevation - stop immediately. Rest 3-5 days. Consult a sports medicine provider. Mild soreness in forearm and shoulder is normal. Sharp, localized pain is not. Protect the arm first.
