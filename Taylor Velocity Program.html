These principles are drawn from research-backed softball throwing programs. Mechanics first, load second. Never throw through sharp pain.
⚡
Intent Is Everything
Throwing at 90-100% intent is required to build velocity. Lazy throws build lazy arm speed. Every max-effort throw should feel like Taylor is throwing through the mitt. Cue: "Rip through the ball."
⚖️
Overload + Underload Balance
Use the 2:1 ratio: two heavy throws per regular-ball throw. Underload (100g) trains fast-twitch arm speed. Overload (250-450g) builds strength and deceleration. Together they expand the velocity envelope on both ends.
🦵
Velocity Starts With the Legs
Outfield throwing velocity is generated ground up: foot, hip, torso, shoulder, arm. The crow-hop must be aggressive and rhythmic. Uphill shoulders, explosive hip fire, and a long stride all add mph before the arm ever fires.
🎯
Glove Arm is a Velocity Tool
The glove arm acts as a crank. Pulled powerfully back and down through hip rotation, it accelerates the throwing arm. Most youth players leave 2-4 mph here. Cue: "Pull the glove into your hip."
🔄
Pronation Means a Safe Arm
Teach Taylor to pronate through release (thumb rotates down after release). This is both a velocity mechanism and injury prevention. Supination bleeds speed and stresses the elbow. Cue: "Show your thumb to the ground."
📏
Long Toss on a Line
Long toss works best on a low, flat trajectory a cutoff man could intercept. High-arc long toss increases elbow stress without the velocity benefit. Chase distance on a line, not height.
🛡️
Arm Care is Non-Negotiable
Before every session: band external rotations 2x15, internal rotations 2x12, Ws 2x12, shoulder circles. After every session: cross-body stretch, sleeper stretch, forearm flexor and extensor. Skipping warmup means skipping gains and risking injury.
📊
Measure Consistently
Same setup every reading: full crow-hop from 55-60 ft on flat ground from the pulldown position. Use Pocket Radar or a radar gun. Inconsistent conditions create misleading data. Log every reading in the Velocity Log tab.
🚫
Red Flags — Stop and Rest
If Taylor reports sharp elbow pain, numbness in fingers, or shoulder pain with arm elevation — stop immediately. Rest 3-5 days. Consult a sports medicine provider. Mild forearm soreness is normal. Sharp localized pain is not.
